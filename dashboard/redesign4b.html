About

how it works

Apex is a concentrated, rules-based momentum portfolio — run in public. Ten names, equal weight at entry, no hedging. Winners are held as long as they trend; a position is sold the moment it breaks — closing below its long-term moving average, or falling hard from its recent high. The freed cash rotates into the next hottest name in a confirmed uptrend.

Every decision is dated and shown, the wins and the scars alike. It's paper-tracked: there's no brokerage connection, so you place your own trades. NAV is price return — it excludes dividends — to mirror how a copy-trading platform scores a portfolio. Dividends are tracked apart, never folded into the headline number.

Where the positions come from

selection

Names are chosen mechanically, not by gut. A universe of liquid, large-cap stocks is ranked by trend strength — recent momentum and whether the name trades above its long-term moving average. When a slot opens, it's filled by the highest-ranked stock in a confirmed uptrend that we don't already hold. Exits are just as mechanical: a trend break below the 200-day line, or a deep drawdown from the high.

The Top 34 book is deliberately simpler — the 34 largest Nasdaq-100 companies by market cap, equal-weighted and held, with a light rebalance once a month. One portfolio picks; the other just owns the giants. Side by side they answer one question: does concentration and timing beat simply holding the index leaders?

Ten names. One benchmark. Zero hindsight.
